I-MAT PROJECT

I-MAT Project

Main Objective

To empower the capacity of Latin Amercan HEIs (Colombia, Peru and Brazil) to attract students and academic and non-academic staff to mobility initiatives, through digital and innovative approaches.

Specific Objectives

  • O1: To bring together key actors in European Countries, responsible for international mobility and cooperation;
  • O2: To better understand the challenges that these three countries are facing to attract students and academic and non-academic staff to mobility initiatives and its exacerbation by the COVID-19 pandemic;
  • O3: Filter the best practices/strategies used in European HEIs to attract the targeted group to mobility initiatives, through digital and innovative approaches;
  • O4: Support Latin American (LA) partner countries in addressing the challenges that their HEIs are facing to attract the focus group to mobility initiatives;
  • O5: Address concrete recommendations and good practices to those involved in mobility initiatives;
  • O6: Create and implement a training framework to strengthen the LA HEIs capacity to attract the targeted group to mobility initiatives, through digital and innovative approaches.

Workpackages:

Project management and coordination

Leader: ULisboa

Objectives:

  • Implement the necessary financial and administrative management of the project.
  • Cary out quality evaluation reporting and follow-up on the of the project and its activities.

Preparation – Status report based on pre-identified conditions and needs

Leader: Vilnius Tech

Objectives:

  • Produce a status report based on pre-identified conditions and needs of the involved LA countries about the participation of students, academic and non-academic staff in International Mobility.

Development – Innovative tools to attract students, academic and non-academic staff for International Mobility

Leader: UCSP

Objectives:

  • Create the Catalogue of Tools of the best European practices to attract students, academic and non-academic staff for international mobility, operated by the European partner universities.
  • Adapt the Catalogue of Tools to the targeted higher education institutions – Latin America (first version).
  • Organize and implement the Pilot-Workshop on the first version of the Catalogue of Tools.

Development – International Mobility Interactive App “I-MOB”

Leader: AGH-UST

Objectives:

  • Design and create an innovative app to promote the attractiveness of students, academic and non-academic staff for international mobility.

Development – Training programmes

Leader: Ulisboa

Objectives:

  • Create and implement a training framework to strengthen the LA HEIs capacity in attracting students and academic and non-academic staff to mobility initiatives, through digital and innovative approaches.

Implementation - Activities to attract students, academic and non-academic staff for International Mobility

Leader: UFRN

Objectives:

  • Structure and implement the innovative activity “Mobility Labs” in the LA HEIs to attract students, academic and non-academic staff for International Mobility.
  • Structure and implement the innovative activity “International Mobility Exhibitions” in the LA HEIs to attract students, academic and non-academic staff for International Mobility.

Impact & Dissemination – Project Promotional Content, Dissemination and Sustainability

Leader: Uniandes

Objectives:

  • Micro dissemination level: identify local primary target users (partner HEI’s actors) - students, administrative and academic staff, with roles related to internationalization.
  • Macro dissemination level: identify the broader set of actors (Governmental bodies, National University Networks, Universities within the same country/region, Private organizations), that will be reached by the publicizing all the project outcomes through national events, national and international conferences, newsletters, press releases and the Project website.
